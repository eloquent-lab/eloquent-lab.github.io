# Evaluating AI-generated Reading Items

The PISA reading assessment measures the reading literacy of 15-year-old students based on the OECD’s reading framework. This theoretical framework forms the basis for evaluating submitted assessment items. Reading literacy encompasses a range of cognitive processes, including searching for and accessing information, understanding and interpreting texts, and evaluating and reflecting on sources and text content. Items are designed to target a wide range of disciplinary content and include different text types (i.e. literary, informational, persuasive and argumentative, conversational). Varying levels of difficulty arise from task characteristics (e.g., question complexity, response mode), text characteristics (e.g., length of the text, number of sources), and the interaction between text and task characteristics (e.g., alignment between question and text, location of target information).

Submissions will consist of a full test form, covering about one hour of testing time. The submissions will be evaluated by experts at both the item level and at the level of the test form. At the item level, experts will assess the phrasing, content, and alignment between the text and the question. They will also verify whether the declared cognitive process and difficulty level is appropriate. Below are some rough guiding questions.


*	Does the item measure the cognitive process it targets?
*	Is the stimulus text appropriate for 15-year-olds and relevant across cultures and languages? 
*	Are questions and answer options phrased naturally, succinct, unambiguous and appropriate for 15-year-olds? Are task requirements clear and rely solely on the stimulus texts? 

At the test form level, submissions are judged by how well the set of items taken together reflect the distribution of cognitive processes in PISA, elaborated below. 
---

## Distribution by Processes

| Broad cognitive processes          | 2029 cognitive processes                       | Share of items |
|------------------------------------|------------------------------------------------|----------------|
| Search and access (25%)            | Accessing information within a text            | 15%            |
|                                    | Searching for and selecting relevant texts     | 10%            |
| Understand and interpret (45%)     | Representing literal meaning                   | 15%            |
|                                    | Understanding and interpreting a single source | 15%            |
|                                    | Understanding and interpreting multiple sources| 15%            |
| Evaluate and reflect (30%)         | Assessing quality and veracity                 | 20%            |
|                                    | Reflecting on content and form                 | —              |
|                                    | Detecting and handling conflict                | 10%            |


Moreover, experts will consider whether the items show a reasonable variety across text source, literary type, response formats and text length. Each test form has to include all types of items listed below – for example, it has to include both single source and multiple source texts. They should broadly reflect the distribution below.


## Distribution by Item Dimensions


**Distribution by item dimensions**

| Dimension        | Type                         | Share | Description                                                                                                                                                                                                        |
|------------------|------------------------------|-------|--------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| **Source**       | Single-source                | 79%   | Texts by the same author, published at the same time and through the same channel. Multi-page sites, long texts with subsections, or texts with embedded sources remain single source if these conditions are met. |
|                  | Multiple-source              | 21%   | Texts that differ by author, publication time or communication channel. Multiple-source texts may also appear together on a single page.                                                                           |
| **Literary type**| Literary                     | 18%   | Narrative texts with characters and a sequence of events. Includes fictional literature, reports and news stories.                                                                                                 |
|                  | Informational                | 47%   | Texts that convey factual, conceptual, or procedural information about the world. This includes descriptions, explanations, text summaries and minutes, and text interpretations.                                  |
|                  | Persuasive and argumentative | 16%   | Texts that explore ideas and claims to convince readers and answer a “why” question.                                                                                                                               |
|                  | Conversational               | 10%   | Interactions between individuals or with AI agents, often personal and to achieve a specific purpose.                                                                                                              |
| **Response Format** | Multiple Choice           | 65%   | Selection of one or more correct answers among a list.                                                                                                                                                             |
|                  | Open Response                | 35%   | Responses requiring students to provide an answer and rationale but without a focus on writing.                                                                                                                    |
| **Text length**  | Short                        | 33%   | Less than 200 words                                                                                                                                                                                                |
|                  | Medium                       | 33%   | Between 200 and 500 words                                                                                                                                                                                          |
|                  | Long                         | 33%   | More than 500 words                                                                                                                                                                                                |

Different levels of difficulty arise from the combination of these dimensions and cognitive processes. The items should show a reasonable variation in difficulty levels.
