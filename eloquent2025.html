Assessing the quality generative language models

The ELOQUENT evaluation lab experiments with new evaluation methods for generative language models to meet some of the challenges in the path from laboratory to application. The intention of the lab is to explore the following important characteristics of generative language model quality:

  1. Trustworthiness: a many-faceted notion which involves topical relevance and truthfulness, discourse competence, reasoning in language, controllability, and robustness across varied input.
  2. Multi-linguality and cultural fit: the suitability of a language model for some cultural and linguistic area.
  3. Self-assessment: the reliability of a language model to assess the quality of itself or some other language model, using as little human effort as possible.
  4. Limits of language models: the delimitation of world knowledge and generative capacity.

Planned tasks:
  • Dictionary Definition Task
    • , testing the capability of a generative language model to select the correct terminological definition from a list of sham and correct choices.
  • Voight-Kampff Task
    • , a continuation from the 2024 task on generating text that appears to be authored by humans.
  • Robustness task
    • , as an evolved version of the 2024 task, on the capability of a generative languag emodel to handle linguistic and cultural input variation consistently.
  • For 2025, we introduce two new more demanding tasks: Preference Score Prediction and Reasoning. These tasks will involve more complex interaction with the system than the previous three tasks and the organisers will proceed by early involvement of teams that develop language models to ensure takeup.
